What actually is a BCG Matrix?

01.

Are you trying to make high-level decisions about the future of your company and that can feel overwhelming? It often requires dozens of meetings to set up special committees which may take months or even years before committing to a path.

However, in the 21st century, the first mover’s advantage is more important than ever. Investing your time and resources efficiently requires prioritizing quickly without letting biases cloud your judgment.

That’s where the BCG (Boston Consulting Group) Matrix comes in. It is a portfolio management tool that assists your organization in setting priorities among several businesses or products in order to get the greatest long-term outcomes.

02.

BCG matrix at 360 Immerse analyze a business’s products to support with long-term strategic planning. It allows companies to identify new growth opportunities and decide how to invest for the future based on factors such as market size, profit margins, operating costs, and cash flow.

The matrix at 360 Immerse utilizes the two metrics to aid you in roughly estimating the profitability of a firm.

Relative Market Share

Your share of the market compared to your largest competitor.

Market Growth Rate

The rate at which a certain market grows in revenues compared to the previous year.

03.

Simplify complex decision-making about investment and business to maximize growth potential and profitability.

  • Prioritize the right business or product.

    Our goal is to establish first-mover advantage by creating new winning products and business opportunities rather than focusing on dying products or cash cows.

  • Eliminate wasteful spending

    In order to reduce your losses, we can identify businesses that have limited growth potential and eliminate unnecessary services.

  • Relative Market Share

    By investing in high-growth markets while maintaining healthy cash cows, we will better balance your company's investment of capital and effort across your whole portfolio.

A great starting point to ensure your investment priorities match your strategic objectives over the long term

Vision - Business aspiration.

Mission - Why the company exists & what the founders envisioned.

Objectives - Result that a company aims to achieve.

Strategy - Long term plan designed to achieve objectives.

Approach - Methodology to execute the long-term plan.

Tactics - Smaller, focused action plans that support the overall strategy.
